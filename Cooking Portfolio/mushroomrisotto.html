MUSHROOM RISOTTO

Serves: 4

Time: 30 minutes

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium shallot, finely diced
  • 1 lb mushrooms (button or shiitake), sliced
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1 1/2 cups arborio rice
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 6 cups chicken stock
  • 1 cup parmesan cheese, grated
  • 1/2 lemon, squeezed
  • salt (to taste)
  • pepper (to taste)

RECIPE PREPARATION

Bring chicken stock in a saucepan to a simmer, keeping a lid on it, on a side burner. In a separate and large stock pot or Dutch oven, bring olive oil to a shimmer over medium heat.

Sauté the shallot until golden brown, then add the garlic, mushrooms and butter. Continue to sauté until you see the mushrooms start to turn golden, as well. Pour the rice in, and toast it in the mixture, stirring constantly for about 1-2 minutes.

Pour in the white wine to deglaze the pan; make sure to scrape the bottom of the pan. Add in the rosemary and thyme at this point.

Using a ladle, pour in the hot chicken stock one ladle’s worth at a time. Stir constantly until absorbed. Once absorbed, add another ladle’s worth of stock and continue this process until almost all of the stock is used (making sure to keep it warm on the side burner).

Once the rice becomes tender and cooked through, take off the heat and stire in the parmesan cheese, lemon juice, and season with salt and pepper to your liking.

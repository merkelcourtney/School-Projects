PORK TENDERLOIN W/ PAN SAUCE

Serves: 4

Time: 1 hour

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 tbsp canola or vegetable oil
  • 2 lb pork tenderloin (typically two pieces)
  • 3 tsp salt (plus more to taste)
  • 2 tsp black pepper (plus more to taste)
  • 2 tsp ground paprika
  • 1 small shallot, finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup Belgian-style ale (like New Belgium Fat Tire)
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken stock
  • 2 tsp dried thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter

RECIPE PREPARATION

Preheat the oven to 450ºF. Heat an oven-safe pan over medium high heat with the neutral oil. Combine the salt, pepper and paprika into a rub, then pat all over the tenderloins, making sure to cover evenly.

Once the oil in the hot pan is shimmering, carefully lay the tenderloins into the pan, making sure not to overcrowd, but they can bend to fit in the pan better. Let sear for about 2 minutes, before putting the pan into the oven. Roast for 10 minutes.

It is easiest to take the pan out of the oven, then flip the tenderloins over and place back into the pan being careful to not overcrowd again. Place them back into the oven for about 15 more minutes, or until the internal temperature of the tenderloins reaches 140ºF. Transfer the tenderloins to a plate or cutting board and cover with foil (it will continue to raise in temperature until your pork is at a medium doneness).

Remove some of the oil and rendered pork fat out of the pan, until you have about 1 tbsp left, and place over medium to medium high heat. Add the shallot and sauté for about 3 minutes, then add garlic and sauté both for about 3 minutes more.

Pour the beer into the pan, stirring pretty constantly, until it becomes a syrup-like consistency - this may take around 5 minutes. Add the chicken stock, thyme and bay leaves, then continue to stir constantly over high heat until the sauce has reduced by about half - this may take around 10 minutes. Take off the heat, remove bay leaves, then add the cold butter, and stir until the sauce becomes even thicker and emulsifies.

Slice tenderloins on the diagonal into 1/2” pieces with a sharp knife, then serve the slices with the sauce spooned over the top.

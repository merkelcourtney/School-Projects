What is deglazing?

What is deglazing?

A fancy word for getting the flavor off the bottom of the pot or pan you just seared something in. Once you're done searing the meat, if you pour something like broth or wine into the pan and begin to scrape, the flavor begins to impart itself into the liquid, multiplying the flavor.

How do I know my meat isn't going to make me sick?

How do I know my meat isn't going to make me sick?

Get a meat thermometer! Make sure to stick it into the thickest part of the meat and make sure it reaches the FDA-approved temperature for the meat you are cooking. Chicken needs to be 165º internally, pork needs to be at least 145º internally and steak can range anywhere from 125º and up. Cook your ground beef til there's no pink remaining, though. That is, unless you're making burgers, in which case I would try 160º.

When should I use butter or oil in a pan?

When to use butter versus oil?

Butter is great for melting and cooking in (like a meat or veggie) when you aren't using it over high heat for a long amount of time. Butter can be burned fairly easy, so it's not a great idea to put it in a ripping hot oven. For cooking over high heat or in a super hot oven, I recommend using a neutral oil like canola, vegetable or grapeseed which have higher smoke points (which means it takes a higher temp to burn them). Olive oil tends to be a happy medium when in doubt!

What utensils do I use in a nonstick pan?

What utensils do I use in a nonstick pan?

Never ever EVER use something metal or abrasive! Wooden or plastic utensils are safe and won't scratch off the coating, which could put the nasty chemicals in your food.

If a recipes calls for wine, how do I choose which one?

If a recipe calls for wine, how do I choose which one?

Recipes will typically tell you what category (like red or white), or may even be specific with a type (like Cabernet Sauvignon or Pinot Grigio). It's best to use what they specifically call for if it is noted. However, if it is vague like "white wine", use something that you would feel comfortable drinking (cheapest wine equals cheapest tasting food) and make sure that it matches the flavor profile of the dish. Cooking something savory? Be sure to get a dry wine, as to not add sweetness where it doesn't belong. Making something that benefits from sweet notes? Consider using semi-dry or a sweet wine – just keep in mind that as the liquid condenses, the sweeter the flavor. In my opinion, it's always best to choose the drier side when you don't know what to pick.

Should you salt pasta water?

Should you salt pasta water?

Yes yes YES! It's a common saying... your pasta water should be salty like the sea. Bring your water to a boil first as putting salt in beforehand can marginally slow down how long it takes to boil (*~SCIENCE~*). Don't feel too bad putting 1/4 cup of salt in several quarts of water. It flavors your pasta as it cooks!

I have no idea the best way to marinade... help!

I have no idea the best way to marinade... help!

Follow this rule of thumb: salt, fat, acid and some sweet. Make sure to put it in a container or bag that won't break or absorb the marinade as it will sit there for a while. Coat what you're marinating in a good amount of fat to coat (like olive oil), and put in half the amount of that fat in acid (like lemon juice or apple cider vinegar). Remember to add in your pepper or other dried spices, and just drizzle in a small amount of sweet (like brown sugar, honey or maple syrup). Salt can be added in the form of plain salt or more creative like soy sauce. You can always google ratios if you need help! Meats can marinade pretty safely for up to 2 days in the fridge, but about an hour is all you need! The flavor doesn't increase a ton after that first hour of marinating.

Do I wash chicken before cooking!

Do I wash chicken before cooking?

NO! It's kind of an old thing that our grandparents and parents were taught, but if you're cooking your chicken to the correct internal temperature (165º), then that liquid that leaks off during cooking won't have any bacteria any more! In fact, rinsing it increases the chance that you're spreading salmonella all over your sink that could spread to other things later.

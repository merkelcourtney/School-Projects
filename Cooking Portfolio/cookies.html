CHOCOLATE CHUNK OATMEAL TOFFEE COOKIES

Makes: about 20 large cookies

Time: 2 hours

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup salted butter (2 sticks)
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup old-fashioned oats
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • 1 1/3 cup brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups Guittard 66% Organic Dark Chocolate wafers
  • 1 cup Heath Bits O' Brickle toffee bits
  • Maldon flaky sea salt

RECIPE PREPARATION

In a sauce pan, heat the sticks of butter of medium to medium high heat. Swirl around as it heats, immediately removing from the heat once the butter solids start to turn a golden color. This should take about 6-7 minutes.

After the butter cools to a not-super-hot temperature, combine with brown sugar in the mixer. Add eggs one at a time, and add in the vanilla. Beat this mixture on high speed until it becomes lighter in color, about 1-2 minutes.

In a separate bowl, combine flour, oats, baking soda and salt. Gradually add this dry mixture to the wet mixture, mixing just to combine. Stir in the chocolate wafers and toffee.

Place the bowl, covered in the fridge for 30-45 minutes (this lets the flour absorb the wet mixture fully). Preheat the oven to 375ÂºF.

Using a cookie or ice cream scoop, portion out balls of dough onto a parchment paper-lined baking sheet about 2-3 inches apart, as they will spread in the oven. Sprinkle these with sea salt before placing in the oven. Bake for 10 minutes, and cool on a wire rack.

SWEET ITALIAN MEATBALLS

Serves: 4

Time: 2 hours

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 lb 80% lean ground beef
  • 1/2 lb sweet Italian pork sausage
  • 1/2 large sweet onion, diced, divided evenly into two bowls
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced, divided evenly into two bowls
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1/3 cup Italian breadcrumbs
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup whole milk Ricotta cheese
  • 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 1/4 cup Italian parsley, chopped
  • 1 tsp salt (more to season sauce later)
  • 1 tsp black pepper (more to season sauce later)
  • 1 can Cento crushed tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • Basil leaves
  • Olive oil

RECIPE PREPARATION

Heat a large deep skillet or Dutch oven with a small coating of olive oil over medium high heat.

In a large bowl, combine the beef, pork, 1/4 cup of the onion, 2 cloves of the garlic, the breadcrumbs, egg, Ricotta, Parmesan cheese, parsley, salt and pepper using your hands until a homogenous mixture forms. Once it seems well mixed, do not keep mixing, as you can risk overworking the meatballs, making them too dense.

Form into 1 1/2 inch balls, and place on a baking sheet or other surface to keep them organized. This recipes creates approximately 30 small meatballs.

Into the hot pan, add half the batch of meatballs (as to not overcrowd the pan) and sauté for a couple minutes on all sides, until they are brown. Remove from pan, then repeat with the second batch. They do not need to be fully cooked, as they will cook in the sauce later.

Remove excess oil with a paper towel, leaving just enough to sauté the rest of your onions and garlic (and red pepper flakes if using) for about 3-4 minutes, or until softened. Pour in the crushed tomatoes, then fill half the can with water and add to the pot. Remember to scrape the bottom to get the flavor from browning the meatballs. At this point, add the brown sugar, stir to mix well, then add the meatballs into the pot. Place the basil leaves in the sauce to impart flavor.

Simmer over medium to medium low heat for about 1 1/2 hours, with the lid only partially covering letting steam escape. Remember to stir every once in a while to check for burning. Once the sauce has reduced by about half, go back in with salt and pepper to season to taste. Serve as meatballs sandwiches or with pasta!

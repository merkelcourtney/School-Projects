Brown Butter and Toffee
Chocolate Chip Cookies

Why make this?

I’ve been a baking enthusiast since before I can even remember. In fact, you can find a picture of me trying to help my mom in the kitchen with an electric mixer at age two. All this to say, that in some twenty-odd years, I’ve made my way around the kitchen and baked everything from cupcake flops to experimental macarons. But the pinnacle of baking — the gold standard if you will — is a kick-ass chocolate chip cookie. Sure, Nestle’s Tollhouse premade dough or recipe somehow always hits the spot, but if you want to distinguish yourself as a competent baker, you’ve got to have a unique and successful chocolate chip cookie recipe in your toolkit. Well, for me, this is mine.

From the way the saltiness hits your palate, to the large pockets of chocolate, and everything in between, you can expect this to become a recipe that your friends and family beg you to make for every event. Part of what makes it so successful is leaving the dough to sit and refrigerate, absorbing and melding all the flavors, beating until pale for a great texture and finishing with the salt. Don’t skip over these steps!

The Recipe

Servings: makes 20
Time: 1.5 hours

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ¾ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 cup (packed) dark brown sugar
  • ⅓ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 1.4-oz chocolate toffee bars (preferably Skor), chopped into ¼-inch pieces
  • 1 ½ cups chocolate wafers (disks, preferably 72% cacao)
  • Flaky sea salt (Maldon)

Directions

  1. Cook butter in a medium saucepan over medium heat, stirring often, until it foams, then browns, 5–8 minutes. Scrape into a large bowl and let cool slightly.
  2. Whisk flour, baking soda, and kosher salt in a medium bowl.
  3. Add brown sugar and granulated sugar to browned butter. Using an electric mixer on medium speed, beat until incorporated, about 1 minute. Add eggs and vanilla, increase mixer speed to medium-high, and beat until mixture lightens and begins to thicken, about 1 minute. Reduce mixer speed to low; add dry ingredients and beat just to combine. Mix in toffee pieces and chocolate wafers with a wooden spoon or a rubber spatula. Let dough sit at room temperature at least 30 minutes to allow the flour to hydrate. Dough will look very loose at first, but will thicken as it sits.
  4. Place a rack in middle of oven; preheat to 375°. Using a 1 ½-oz. ice cream scoop, portion out 10 balls of dough and place on a parchment-lined baking sheet, spacing about 3" apart (you can also form dough into ping pong–sized balls with your hands). Do not flatten; cookies will spread as they bake. Sprinkle with sea salt.
  5. Bake cookies until edges are golden brown and firm but centers are still soft, 9–11 minutes. Let cool on baking sheets 10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack and let cool completely. Repeat with remaining dough and a fresh parchment-lined baking sheet to make 10 more cookies.

Do Ahead

Cookie dough can be made 3 days ahead; cover and chill. If formed into balls, wait to add salt until just before baking. Let dough come to room temperature before putting into the oven.
